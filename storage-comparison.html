Non-Negotiable
  • Climate controlled — interior, not just exterior shell
  • Stable temperature year-round, art-safe
  • Ground floor or genuine freight elevator for rolling racks
  • 10×15 minimum · 10×20 preferred
  • No Public Storage
  • Active access — working archive, not set-and-forget
Art Contents
  • 3 boxed canvases: ~9×9 ft · ~7×7 ft · ~4×5 ft
  • 1 rolled painting (large, lightweight)
  • 1 box ~3×2×4 ft with smaller works
  • All professionally packed, non-reactive wrap
  • Ceiling height must clear 9+ ft stored upright
  • Stored on 4×4 runners, raised off floor, slight lean for airflow
Working Archive Contents
  • ~40 bins, labeled, stackable ~5 high
  • Metro shelving on locking casters
  • 2 professional rolling racks — actively used
  • Backdrop tubes / fabric rolls
  • Steamer · small appliances · sewing machine
  • Herman Miller chair + folding table
Target Layout — U-Shape
  • Back wall: canvases upright on runners, raised off floor
  • Side wall 1: bins stacked in columns
  • Side wall 2: shelving + equipment
  • Front / corner: backdrops, fabric, steamer
  • Center: rolling racks + chair + table
  • Clear working aisle — nothing needs to be unloaded to access anything
Questions to Ask Every Facility
  • Ceiling height in 10×15 and 10×20 specifically?
  • Ground floor available? If not, freight or standard elevator?
  • Hallway width — two rolling racks side by side?
  • Access hours — evenings? Sundays?
  • Temperature range maintained, year-round spec?
  • Loading: dock, drive-up, or lobby ramp?
  • Unit availability or waitlist?
  • Month-to-month vs. long-term pricing?
Decision Priority Order
  • 1. Climate stability — protect the art
  • 2. Layout usability — working archive, not just storage
  • 3. Ease of access — racks, bins, repeated visits
  • 4. Proximity to Blue Hill / parkways / Saugerties
  • 5. Right unit size available
  • 6. Local / regional operator preference
  • 7. Price
